Website Proposal:

Purpose and Goals

My purpose in building a resume website is to have a reliable and effective tool that showcases my skills, abilities, and achievements to potential employers, clients, and other professionals. By presenting my resume/portfolio in a website format, I can demonstrate my skills and abilities in a more comprehensive way, which can help me stand out from the competition and gain more opportunities. My hope for my live URL is https://www.emilycannon.com.


Target Audience

As a future web developer, I envision my resume website as a platform to exhibit my skills and expertise to potential future employers and clients. I aim to create a website that is professional and polished and highlights my current projects and achievements in a clear and concise manner, with room to share future projects and experiences. I would be sharing my website's URL with future potential employers, which I hope will provide them with a better understanding of my qualifications and expertise. Additionally, I would want to share my URL with fellow professionals in the web development field in the future for networking opportunities or potential collaborations on projects.


Competitor Analysis with Examples

Many people create and use resume websites for a variety of careers. However, in order to stand out from the sea of other resume websites, I'll have to make my resume site unique to myself and have my projects easily accessible in an aesthetic format. The site will need to be easily navigated with the important information presented in an appealing fashion, where it will possibly catch someone's interest or attention quickly.

Content Strategy

The visual content for my resume website will mainly consist of images and links to my own projects, and I'll strive to use images that are high-quality and visually appealing that show my best work. These images may be graphic design projects I have done or screenshots from webpages I have built; if I showcase some of the small webpages I have built, I will include links to them as well. Also, if I end up using any GIFs or images from the web, I'll always cite them to give proper credit.


Navigation

My resume website will contain about five different web pages: the home page, the about page, the graphic design project page and the web projects page under "Portfolio", and the contact page. The most important information for the viewer, if, for example, they are a potential employer, would be the About page, where they could see my experiences, education, etc., and for a potential client, the portfolio page would be most important because they could see my past work and decide whether they would want to work with me.
The hierarchy of my website is as follows:

  • Home Page:
    • Introduce myself and the purpose of the webpage, and include a “Resume” button under the text that opens my LinkedIn page in a new tab.
  • About:
    • Bio: This page will provide more in-depth information (than the home page) about who I am and what I do. I may also share my education and experiences (or leave that space for that information in the future). I may also include a "Skills" section on this page with a list of my existing skills.
  • Portfolio:
    • Graphic design projects: I would like to showcase my graphic design projects via a tile layout and possibly create each tile where they 'flip over' with a simple cursor hover trigger to show a quick description of said projects.
    • Web projects: showcase a few of my web projects via links and screenshots and possibly include a small description of each project via a dropdown feature.
  • Contact:
    • I'd like to create a simple contact form with text input boxes for viewers to type in their name, email, and/or phone number and the message they want to send. I may also possibly include another link to my LinkedIn as a second line of contact, where the viewer of my website could message me there instead.

Font Families

I have chosen Rokkitt for the headings and Roboto for the body of my website. I decided to go for a sans-serif font like Roboto for the body as it makes the content more readable. On the other hand, I also chose Rokkitt because it is a serif font that creates a nice contrast between the headings and the body, making the content more interesting for the viewer. To create some contrast between the level one and level two headings, I decided to italicize the level two headings in the same Rokkitt font as the level one headings Overall, the combination of these two fonts creates contrast and interest in the content while also maintaining good readability. Additionally, I chose these two fonts because Roboto was trending on Google Fonts, so I used it for the body. This choice led me to select Rokkitt font for the level one and level two headings; I was inspired to choose Rokkitt by a Figma article that displayed the best fonts that work well with Roboto.
